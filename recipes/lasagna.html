Lasagna

Return to Home

Description

The ultimate comfort food: A steaming, homemade lasagna, fresh out of the oven. It’s baked to a perfect golden-brown and fills the room with the mouth-watering scent of savory bolognese and herbs. That magical moment when the melted mozzarella stretches into gooey strings promises pure coziness. Garnished with fresh basil—a slice of Italian warmth on a plate.

Ingredients

The Meat Sauce (Ragù)

  • Ground Beef: (Or a mix of beef and pork)
  • Olive Oil: For sautéing.
  • Aromatics: Onions and garlic (finely chopped).
  • Tomatoes: Crushed tomatoes, tomato purée (passata), and tomato paste.
  • Herbs: Dried oregano, dried basil, and perhaps a bay leaf.
  • Seasoning: Salt, black pepper, and a pinch of sugar (to balance the acidity).

The Béchamel (White Sauce)

  • Butter: Unsalted is usually best.
  • All-Purpose Flour: To make the roux.
  • Whole Milk: For a creamy texture.
  • Nutmeg: A pinch of ground nutmeg (essential for the classic flavor).
  • Salt & Pepper.

Assembly & Topping

  • Lasagna Sheets: (Egg pasta sheets, either pre-cooked or oven-ready).
  • Mozzarella Cheese: Shredded (for the layers) and fresh slices (for the top to get that stringy "cheese pull").
  • Parmesan Cheese: Grated (for the savory, golden crust).
  • Fresh Basil: Whole leaves for the garnish on top.

Steps

Step 1: Make the Meat Sauce (Ragù)

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and garlic and sauté until soft (about 5 minutes).
  2. Add the ground beef. Break it up with a spoon and cook until browned. Drain any excess fat if necessary.
  3. Stir in the tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, and tomato purée.
  4. Add the herbs (oregano, basil, bay leaf) and season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of sugar.
  5. Reduce heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for at least 20–30 minutes to let the flavors meld.

Step 2: Make the Béchamel Sauce

  1. Melt the butter in a medium saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Whisk in the flour and cook for 1–2 minutes (do not let it brown) to create a roux.
  3. Gradually pour in the milk while whisking constantly to avoid lumps.
  4. Simmer gently, stirring frequently, until the sauce thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon.
  5. Remove from heat and stir in the nutmeg, salt, and pepper.

Step 3: Assembly

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish (this prevents sticking).
  3. Layer 1: Place lasagna sheets over the sauce. Top with a layer of meat sauce, a drizzle of béchamel, and a sprinkle of grated Parmesan and shredded Mozzarella.
  4. Repeat Layers: Repeat this process (Pasta -> Meat -> Béchamel -> Cheese) until you fill the dish (usually 3 or 4 layers).
  5. Top Layer: Finish with a final layer of pasta, cover completely with the remaining béchamel (to keep pasta soft), and top generously with the remaining Mozzarella and Parmesan.

Step 4: Bake

  1. Cover the dish with aluminum foil (tip: spray the foil with oil so the cheese doesn't stick to it).
  2. Bake for 25 minutes covered.
  3. Remove the foil and bake for another 15–20 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.

Step 5: Rest & Serve

  1. Important: Let the lasagna rest for 10–15 minutes after taking it out of the oven. This allows the layers to set so it doesn't fall apart when you cut it.
  2. Garnish with fresh basil leaves just before serving.

Enjoy your meal!
